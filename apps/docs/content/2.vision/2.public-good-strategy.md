# Public-Good Strategy

Trellis should be open infrastructure first. The protocol, core data model, ontologies, trajectory format, and reference corpus are the commons. Hosted services, polished applications, institutional tooling, and commercial surfaces are downstream.

## Protocol first

The durable output is not a single app. It is a substrate that domain-specific tools can share:

- Research notebooks
- Coding environments
- Agent workstations
- Educational tools
- Personal knowledge systems
- Institutional provenance systems

## Why this matters

Knowledge work is increasingly AI-assisted, but the record of how work happened is often invisible. Logs are not provenance. Trellis makes operations, decisions, artifacts, and trajectories machine-readable and replayable.

## Business downstream

The commercial surface can be hosted nodes, team tooling, browser gateways, backups, indexes, or domain-specific workspaces. Those products should remain downstream of the open protocol, like GitHub is downstream of Git.
