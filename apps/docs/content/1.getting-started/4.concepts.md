# Concepts

## Entity-Attribute-Value graph

Trellis stores graph state as entities with typed facts and links. This keeps schema evolution flexible while still allowing structured queries.

## Causal operation log

Every mutation should become a durable, inspectable operation. Operations are content-addressed and causally linked so state can be audited, replayed, and synchronized.

## Queryable views

Views are queries over graph state. Lists, boards, dashboards, graph explorers, and agent workspaces should render projections over the same substrate.

## Narrative milestones

Milestones are human-readable checkpoints over a range of operations. They are not speculative plans; they summarize coherent completed work.

## Governance subgraph

Identity, signatures, permissions, capabilities, invites, and revocation should live in the graph rather than in an external authority.

## Sync as replication

Peer sync replicates signed, causally ordered operations between Trellis nodes. Relays and gateways may help with transport, but they do not own primary state.
